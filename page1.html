FIRST YEAR

Here a few pictures that capture my first year of engineering at the University of Toronto!

I successfully coded my first personal website! Through this project, I gained a strong understanding of HTML and CSS, the building blocks of web development. I also learned the patience of troubleshooting and debugging code, which helped to improve my problem solving abilities. To ensure that every element worked as intended, I discovered the signficance of precision and detail that is required in coding.
Thrilled to have led a team of engineers in designing and prototyping a collapsible, modifiable, and durable support structure for backpacking stoves. As the project manager, I coordinated the team's efforts, facilitated client meetings, and ensured timely progress. We also authored a comprehensive engineering report and delivered a polished final presentation, demonstrating our solution to the client.
From setting up oscilloscopes to measuring signal frequencies, and understanding the transient responses in RC circuits, these labs provided me with hands-on experience. Each step, was meticulously documented, which significantly enhanced my practical understanding of AC measurements and circuit dynamics.
Developing the Reversi game in my C course was the most challenging project of the semester. Despite its difficulty, I persevered and successfully completed it. This experience greatly enhanced my problem-solving abilities and deepened my understanding of C programming, marking a key learning milestone.
Creating a word search puzzle in my C course was a challenging yet rewarding experience. I designed

the puzzle to include hidden words in a grid of letters, requiring complex algorithms for word placement and searching. This project honed my skills and significantly enhancing my understanding of C programming.
For our client, my team and I helped design an improved wayfinding system for a historic building's complex layout. We integrated a wall-mounted floor map, interactive digital signs, and a QR-code-accessible map to enhance navigation. This solution aimed to simplify visitor access, reduce staff burden, and blend with the building's neo-Gothic style.
